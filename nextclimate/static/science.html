How do we make these climate change estimates?

The climate is the average of weather over a long time period. While it may get cold every once and a while in Los Angeles, we still think of Los Angeles as having a "mild" climate. To tell the difference between an unusual event and a change in climate, we have to look over a long time period. For this reason, we have to look back to what it was like when our parents were children, or what we expect it to be like when our grandchildren are middle-aged adults. Recent history suggest that the climate has already changed substantially, and looking ahead, future generations face a climate crisis.

To examine what the climate was like during our parent's lifetime, we look at the average of measurements of temperature over 20 years from 1950 -- 1970. Our children's generation will live to see the year 2050, and we use the results of computer simulations for the 20-years 2040 -- 2060. Finally, the estimates for our grandchildren's generation use these same computer simulations for the years 2080 -- 2100. The computer simulations are part of an international scientific effort to understand climate change, described next.

International effort, locally focused

As part of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change's Fourth Assessment Report, climate scientists from around the world contributed their computer simulations to a common data archive. These results have been re-analyzed to a finer spatial resolution to better reflect local climate patterns. Finally, we map these local results to your zip code.

The data presented here are not a forecast of the future. This is impossible. Rather they are a warning of what will likely come to pass if we continue on our current course of action.

What can I do?

When we use fossil fuels, like coal for power plants or gasoline in our car, we add more greenhouse gases to the atmosphere that accelerate the rate of climate change. However, there is much that you can do to help make where you live a better place for your children and grandchildren. Find out how you can lower your emissions and save on your energy bills.
